## Category 1: Business Planning & Strategy
*50 prompts to build, plan, and grow your business with AI*

1. Write a one-page business overview for [company name] that I can give investors. Include: what we do in one sentence, the problem we solve, target customers, revenue model, traction ([metric]), and our unfair advantage.

2. Create a 90-day launch plan for [product name] entering [market]. Break into 3 phases: Pre-launch (days 1–30), Soft launch (31–60), Full launch (61–90). Include 5 specific tasks per phase and KPIs for each phase.

3. Analyze my business idea: [describe idea]. Give me: (1) Market size estimate, (2) Top 3 competitors, (3) My advantages over them, (4) The #1 risk to this business, (5) A first 30-day test to validate real demand before I invest money.

4. Create a Business Model Canvas for [business name] — fill all 9 blocks with specifics: Customer Segments, Value Propositions, Channels, Customer Relationships, Revenue Streams, Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships, Cost Structure.

5. Write a SWOT analysis for [company] in [industry]. Format as a clear 2×2 matrix with 5 items in each quadrant, then write 3 strategic initiatives that turn strengths into opportunities and address the biggest threats.

6. Help me create OKRs for Q[X] [year]. Main focus area: [focus area]. Create 3 Objectives that are ambitious but achievable. For each Objective, write 3–4 measurable Key Results with specific numbers and deadlines.

7. I'm choosing between [Business Idea A] and [Business Idea B]. Compare both on: startup cost, time to first revenue, scalability ceiling, market size, and my skill fit. Score each 1–10 on every dimension and recommend one with clear reasoning.

8. Draft my company's mission statement, vision statement, and 5 core values. Context: [describe company, what we do, who we serve, and what we believe]. Make each value 1–3 words with a one-sentence explanation.

9. Create a one-year strategic roadmap for [company] broken into 4 quarters. For each quarter, define: the strategic theme, 3 major milestones, required resources, and how success will be measured.

10. Write an executive summary for my business plan. Company: [name]. Product/service: [describe]. Problem solved: [describe]. Revenue model: [describe]. Market opportunity: [size]. Current traction: [metrics]. Funding ask: [$X]. Keep it to 400 words.

11. Conduct a competitive analysis for [my product] against these 3 competitors: [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], [Competitor 3]. Compare on: pricing, features, target customer, distribution channel, and brand positioning. Identify the whitespace I can own.

12. Write a competitive positioning map narrative for [product] in the [industry] market. Identify the 2 axes most important to customers (e.g., price vs. quality, speed vs. customization) and explain where I sit and why that's defensible.

13. Create a 'Jobs to Be Done' framework analysis for [product/service]. List 5 functional jobs, 3 emotional jobs, and 3 social jobs customers are hiring my product to do. Then suggest how to message each in marketing.

14. My biggest competitor is [competitor]. Write a complete feature-by-feature comparison table, then write a 'why choose us over them' section I can use on my website. Be honest about where they win and sharp about where we win.

15. Identify the top 5 market trends shaping [industry] over the next 3 years. For each trend, explain: what's driving it, how it affects my business ([describe business]), and one strategic action I should take to capitalize on it.

16. Write a market segmentation analysis for [product/service]. Identify 4 distinct customer segments, describe each (demographics + psychographics + behaviors), rank them by attractiveness, and recommend which to target first and why.

17. Create a blue ocean strategy analysis for [business idea]. What industry assumptions can I eliminate? What factors can I reduce below industry standard? What can I raise above standard? What can I create that the industry has never offered?

18. Analyze Porter's Five Forces for [industry]: Threat of new entrants, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers, threat of substitutes, industry rivalry. Rate each force 1–10 and explain what it means for my business strategy.

19. I'm entering a market dominated by [established player]. Write a market entry strategy that helps me compete without directly fighting their strengths. Include: my beachhead segment, differentiation angle, pricing approach, and first 100 customers acquisition plan.

20. Write a positioning statement and tagline for [company] targeting [customer type]. Formula: 'For [target customer] who [need/want], [company name] is the [category] that [key benefit] because [reason to believe].' Then write 3 tagline options under 8 words each.

21. Create a value proposition canvas for [product]. Map: Customer Jobs (5), Pains (5), Gains (5) on one side, and Pain Relievers (5), Gain Creators (5), Products & Services (3) on the other. Then write a 2-sentence value proposition that connects them.

22. Write a go-to-market strategy for [product] launching in [month/year]. Include: target segment, distribution channels, pricing rationale, sales motion (self-serve/sales-led/channel), launch week activities, and success metrics for the first 90 days.

23. Create a product-market fit checklist for [product]. Include 10 signals I should look for that indicate strong fit (qualitative + quantitative), how to measure each, and what threshold indicates I've achieved PMF.

24. Help me define my Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for [B2B product]. Include: company size, industry, tech stack, business model, growth stage, trigger events that make them buy, and red flags that disqualify a prospect.

25. Write a pricing strategy analysis for [product/service]. Evaluate 4 pricing models (cost-plus, value-based, competitive, freemium) against my context. Recommend one with a specific price point and explain the psychological reasoning.

26. Create a channel strategy for selling [product]. Evaluate these channels: direct sales, partnerships, online marketplace, content/SEO, paid ads, events. Rank them by: reach, cost, control, fit for my audience. Recommend my top 2 and why.

27. Draft a strategic narrative for [company] in the style of a 'We believe' manifesto. Start with the problem in the world, describe the future we're building toward, and explain our unique role in making it happen. Keep it under 300 words.

28. Create a market timing argument for [business idea]. Why is now the right time for this to exist? Reference 3 market conditions, technology shifts, or behavioral changes that make this the ideal moment to launch.

29. Write a customer acquisition strategy for reaching my first 1,000 customers for [product]. Include specific tactics for each phase: 0–100 (direct), 100–500 (referral/content), 500–1,000 (paid). No generic advice — be specific to [product type].

30. Develop a partnership strategy for [company]. Identify 5 types of ideal strategic partners, what each partnership would look like, the mutual value exchange, and a one-paragraph outreach pitch for each partner type.

31. Design a growth strategy for [company] to go from [$X] to [$Y] revenue in [timeframe]. Identify the primary growth lever (acquisition, activation, retention, referral, or revenue). Create a 90-day sprint plan focused solely on that lever.

32. I'm considering pivoting my business from [current model] to [new model]. Help me evaluate this pivot: What evidence suggests I should pivot? What am I keeping vs. changing? What are the risks? Create a 60-day pivot execution plan.

33. Write a land-and-expand sales strategy for my [B2B SaaS/service]. How do I win a small initial contract, deliver fast value, then systematically expand into more seats/departments/budget? Include upsell triggers and timing.

34. Create a referral growth strategy for [business]. Design the incentive structure, the referral ask messaging, the referral landing page copy, and an email sequence that activates referrals from happy customers. All components, not just ideas.

35. Help me identify the one metric that matters most (OMTM) for [business] right now at [stage: idea/launch/growth/scale]. Explain why this metric above all others, how to measure it daily, and what actions move it.

36. Design a product-led growth (PLG) strategy for [SaaS product]. Include: free tier definition, activation event, habit-forming feature, viral loop, and the moment we convert free to paid. Map the full user journey.

37. Write a content-led growth strategy for [company] in [industry]. Identify 3 content pillars, the SEO opportunity, distribution channels, and how content converts to customers. Include a 6-month execution timeline.

38. Create an expansion revenue strategy for my existing customer base of [X customers]. How do I identify upsell/cross-sell opportunities, when to approach, what to offer, and how to frame the conversation without feeling pushy?

39. I'm at [revenue stage]. Write a fundraising vs. bootstrapping analysis for my situation: [describe business]. What are the long-term implications of each path? Which do you recommend and what's the first action step?

40. Design a strategic experiment framework for [company] to test our top 3 growth hypotheses this quarter. For each hypothesis, create: the test design, success metric, sample size/duration, and decision criteria for scaling or killing.

41. Write a one-paragraph 'elevator pitch' for [company] that I can use when someone asks 'What do you do?' Make it conversational, highlight the problem, solution, and traction, and end with an invitation to learn more.

42. Create an investor FAQ document for [company] covering the 10 questions investors always ask: market size, competition, moat, unit economics, team, go-to-market, use of funds, milestones, exit strategy, and biggest risk.

43. Write a problem slide narrative for my pitch deck. Problem: [describe]. Who has it: [target customer]. How big is the pain (use data): [evidence]. Why existing solutions fail: [reason]. Keep it punchy — 3–4 bullet points max.

44. Draft a strategic partnership proposal for approaching [type of company] as a distribution partner for [my product]. Include: mutual value, what I'm asking for, what I'm offering, proof points, and a clear next step.

45. Create a competitive moat analysis for [company]. Identify which of the 7 moats we have or can build: network effects, switching costs, brand, scale advantages, proprietary technology, regulatory licenses, or cost leadership. Rate each 1–5.

46. Write a board update email template for [company stage]. Cover: progress against KPIs, key wins this month, challenges and how we're addressing them, upcoming milestones, asks from the board. Keep it under 600 words with a data table.

47. Create a scenario planning exercise for [company] for the next 12 months. Write 3 scenarios: Bear (things go wrong), Base (expected path), Bull (things go right). For each, describe conditions, revenue outcome, and strategic response.

48. Draft an LOI (Letter of Intent) template for a strategic acquisition conversation with [type of target company]. Include: what we're proposing, valuation approach, key terms, exclusivity period, and next steps. Keep it professional but not legalese.

49. Write a 'Founder Story' narrative for my pitch that connects my personal background to why I'm uniquely suited to solve [problem]. Use the arc: early experience → insight → obsession → company creation. Keep it under 200 words.

50. Create a quarterly business review (QBR) template for [company]. Sections: Executive summary, Revenue metrics (table), Customer metrics, Product milestones, Team updates, Challenges, Q[X+1] priorities, and Asks. Include the key questions each section should answer.
